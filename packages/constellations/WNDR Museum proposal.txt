Constellations @ WNDR Museum — Proposal (Draft)

Overview
Constellations is an interactive, AI-powered “knowledge explorer” that lets guests quickly navigate the Wikipedia information space through an evidence-backed, bipartite graph (People ↔ Events / Ingredients ↔ Recipes / Symptoms ↔ Diseases). It’s designed for low-friction, playful discovery: tap to expand, inspect the “why” behind connections, and follow curiosity.

Why WNDR
WNDR is built around participatory, curiosity-driven experiences. Constellations fits naturally as a “playable” knowledge installation: it’s visually engaging, invites experimentation, and supports both short interactions and longer “I can’t stop clicking” sessions.

Guest experience (what visitors do)
- Start from a featured seed (e.g., an artist, work, movement, or WNDR-related concept)
- Tap to expand the graph and reveal connected entities
- Click edges to see evidence/citations (the “why”)
- Switch between network and timeline views to see a story unfold over time

Proposed collaboration
1) Deploy Constellations on a public-facing touchscreen kiosk (no install; runs in Chrome).
2) Create a WNDR-specific domain pack:
   - A curated seed list: featured artists, exhibits, themes, and “jumping off” concepts.
   - Optional: rotating “Today’s starting points” to align with programming/seasonality.
3) Iterate quickly based on staff/guest feedback (what’s fun, what confuses people, what seeds perform best).

Technical requirements
- Large touchscreen display running a modern Chromium browser (Chrome/Chromium)
- Reliable internet connection (required for live retrieval + AI expansion)
- Optional: keyboard disabled / kiosk mode

Notes / limitations (so expectations are clear)
- Works best for entities that are well-covered by Wikipedia and have stable naming.
- Evidence is shown to support interpretation and verification; the system is designed to reduce hallucinations, but it’s still an AI-assisted experience.

Success signals (simple, measurable)
- Median session length (people often spend 10+ minutes on first use)
- “Depth” of exploration (expansions per session)
- Repeat engagement (do guests return to try a new seed?)
- Qualitative: “surprise discoveries” / “frontier moments” (what did guests find that they didn’t expect?)
